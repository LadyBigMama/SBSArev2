Waiver

Before flying Santa Barbara sites, complete the SBSA registration flow, review local rules, and make sure your waiver requirements are handled.

This page is built from the current SBSA visiting-pilot registration and waiver guidance so the waiver button leads to one place with the practical steps visitors need before they fly.

Registration

Joining the SBSA is the first step. Annual membership is $30 and supports site preservation, public relations, site maintenance, website expenses, La Cumbre weather station expenses, SBSA property taxes on the LZs we own, and local events that promote free flight in Santa Barbara.

Over the last 40+ years, SBSA effort has kept free flight alive at sites like Elings Park, Parma Park, East Beach, More Mesa, The T, Wilcox, Bates, and Skyport. That work depends on members and visiting pilots participating in the registration process.

Waiver Checklist

Pilots visiting Santa Barbara flying sites should complete all of the following before flying:

  • Review the No Landing Zones and site rules on the Flying Sites page.
  • Join the SBSA as part of the registration process.
  • Get a site intro from a local guide or instructor.
  • Read, understand, and follow the local site guidelines for all Santa Barbara areas.
  • Make sure the mountain and/or beach airspace window has been opened before flight.
  • If you are going XC, request the DNL file on Telegram. It is sensitive and shared with visitors on request.

Required Safety Equipment

  • Reserve parachute
  • Helmet
  • 2-meter radio, charged and functional

Telegram and Airspace

Our flying community uses Telegram to communicate conditions, meet times, flight reports, and visitor coordination. Introduce yourself there and ask for current local guidance.

Santa Barbara flying happens near airport airspace. Ask a local guide to open the appropriate airspace window before flying. Failure to contact the tower can put local site access at risk.
