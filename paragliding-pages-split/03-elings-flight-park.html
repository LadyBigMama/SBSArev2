The presence of a local school is mandatory for individual pilots to fly there:


Elings Flight Park is a private property. They ask that only the approved flight schools have access to instruct and coach students and certified pilots. Pilots of any rating are only able to fly or ground handle at this site under the supervision the Elings Flight Park approved flight schools.  

Out of town paragliding schools and instructors need approval before using the Elings Park Training Hill for instruction.  


Student pilots with less than novice hang gliding rating (H2) or novice paragliding rating (P2) must have direct supervision by their USHPA certified, Eling’s Park Flight Park approved, instructor.


Call Fly Above All (805-965-3733), Eagle Paragliding (805-968-0980) or (802-558-6350) for details.


When a school instructor is present, a certified pilot wishing to practice at the hill are expected to:

-Check-in with an on-site instructor. 

-Show current proof of USHPA rating, pay training hill fees, and complete the waiver.

-Wear a helmet anytime they are attached to their hang glider/paraglider.

-Keep a safe physical distance between moving gliders, people and vehicles. 

-Keep in mind that students are flying with you, do not increase their stress level, keep plenty of distance away from them and shout launching when about to do so.

Traffic Regulation:


The entrance gate needs to remain closed at all times. Close the gate behind you every time. The park management is very strict about this.

If you see it open, please close it.


Because of the rough road condition going up, 2wheel drive vehicles should park at the bottom.


Vehicles are not allowed in landing or launching area – they are to remain on the roads or designated parking areas at all times.


All hang gliding and paragliding activities must be in compliance with USHPA hang gliding and paragliding pilot rating, instructor certification programs, and FAR 103.


Please drive slow. Hikers with dogs will be grateful.

Paragliding and hang gliding pilots have the right of way over RC gliders

Assist the instructors in assuring that all other park visitors comply with all park rules.


Each park user is responsible for upholding the best interests of the flight park. Any inappropriate or unsafe conduct (as deemed by any instructor or USHPA certified pilot) may result in a temporary or permanent ban from Elings Flight Park.

There is a lot of poison oak around. Be mindful that landing in bushes can lead to poison oak contamination.
